## 📚 Common Questions

### Q: What's the difference between CS106A and CS106B?
**A**: CS106A is introductory programming in Python (no prerequisites). CS106B is data structures and algorithms in C++ (requires CS106A or equivalent). See [Core Courses](undergraduate/core/README.md).

### Q: Should I take CS106B or CS106X?
**A**: CS106X is accelerated and covers more material. Take CS106X if you have strong programming background. See [Core Courses](undergraduate/core/README.md).

### Q: What's the hardest CS course?
**A**: CS110 (Principles of Computer Systems) is often considered the hardest undergraduate course. CS140 (Operating Systems) and CS224N/CS231N are challenging graduate courses. See [Course Index](COURSE_INDEX.md).

### Q: Can I take graduate courses as an undergrad?
**A**: Yes! Many advanced undergrads take 200-level courses. Check prerequisites and get advisor approval.

### Q: How do I get into Stanford CS grad school?
**A**: 
- **MS**: ~5-7% acceptance rate. Need strong GPA (3.7+), good GRE (optional now), strong letters. See [MS Admissions](graduate/masters/MS_PROGRAM_GUIDE.md#-application-process).
- **PhD**: ~3-4% acceptance rate. Need research experience, publications (preferred), strong letters from researchers. See [PhD Admissions](graduate/phd/PHD_PROGRAM_GUIDE.md#-admission-process).

### Q: Is the MS program funded?
**A**: Not automatically. Some students get TA/RA positions (tuition + stipend). External fellowships available. See [MS Funding](graduate/masters/MS_PROGRAM_GUIDE.md#-funding--financial-aid).

### Q: Is the PhD program funded?
**A**: Yes, 100% of admitted students receive full funding (tuition + $45-50K stipend). See [PhD Funding](graduate/phd/PHD_PROGRAM_GUIDE.md#-funding--stipend).

### Q: What programming languages should I know?
**A**: 
- **Essential**: Python, C/C++, Java
- **Useful**: JavaScript, SQL, MATLAB
- **Specialized**: CUDA (for GPU), Rust (for systems)

### Q: What math do I need?
**A**: 
- **Required**: Calculus, Linear Algebra, Probability, Discrete Math
- **Useful**: Multivariable Calculus, Statistics, Optimization
- See [Core Courses](undergraduate/core/README.md) for specifics

### Q: How much does Stanford CS cost?
**A**: 
- **Undergraduate**: ~$60,000/year tuition + living expenses
- **MS**: ~$81,000 total (45 units × $1,800/unit)
- **PhD**: Free (fully funded)
- See program guides for details

### Q: What jobs can I get with a CS degree?
**A**: 
- Software Engineer: $150K-$220K
- ML Engineer: $160K-$250K
- Research Scientist: $180K-$300K
- Data Scientist: $140K-$200K
- See [Career Information](COMPLETE_CATALOG_SUMMARY.md#-career-information)
