## 🎓 Program Overviews

### Bachelor of Science (BS) in Computer Science

**Duration**: 4 years  
**Total Units**: 120+ units  
**Core Courses**: 11 required courses  
**Electives**: 6-8 courses in specialization track  

**Core Curriculum**:
1. CS106A - Programming Methodology
2. CS106B - Programming Abstractions
3. CS107 - Computer Organization & Systems
4. CS110 - Principles of Computer Systems
5. CS103 - Mathematical Foundations
6. CS109 - Probability for Computer Scientists
7. CS161 - Design and Analysis of Algorithms
8. CS111 - Operating Systems Principles
9. Plus math requirements (MATH 19/20/21, MATH 51)

**Specialization Tracks**:
- Artificial Intelligence
- Systems
- Theory
- Human-Computer Interaction
- Computer Graphics
- Biocomputation
- Information

**Career Outcomes**:
- Software Engineer: $120,000-$180,000
- Data Scientist: $110,000-$160,000
- Product Manager: $130,000-$190,000
- Graduate School: MS/PhD programs

---

### Master of Science (MS) in Computer Science

**Duration**: 1-2 years (3-6 quarters)  
**Total Units**: 45 units  
**Admission Rate**: ~5-7%  
**Average GPA**: 3.8+  

**Requirements**:
- **Foundations**: 5 core areas (can use undergrad courses)
- **Breadth**: 3 courses from different areas
- **Depth**: 21 units in specialization (15+ at 200-level)
- **Electives**: Remaining units

**Specialization Areas**:
1. Artificial Intelligence (most popular)
2. Systems
3. Theory
4. Human-Computer Interaction
5. Computer Graphics
6. Databases & Information Systems
7. Security & Privacy
8. Computational Biology
9. Software Theory
10. Computer & Network Security

**Funding Options**:
- Teaching Assistantships (TA): Tuition + $8,000/quarter
- Research Assistantships (RA): Tuition + stipend
- Fellowships: NSF GRFP, NDSEG, etc.
- Industry Sponsorship: Part-time enrollment

**Career Outcomes**:
- Machine Learning Engineer: $160,000-$250,000
- Research Scientist: $180,000-$300,000
- Software Engineer: $150,000-$220,000
- PhD Programs: ~20% continue to PhD

---

### Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Computer Science

**Duration**: 4-6 years (average 5.5 years)  
**Cohort Size**: 30-35 students/year  
**Admission Rate**: ~3-4%  
**Funding**: 100% of admitted students  

**Requirements**:
- **Coursework**: 135 units minimum
- **Teaching**: 3 quarters of TA work
- **Qualifying Exam**: End of year 2
- **Dissertation**: Original research contribution
- **Defense**: Oral defense of dissertation

**Research Areas**:
1. **Artificial Intelligence**: ML, NLP, Vision, Robotics
2. **Systems**: OS, Networking, Databases, Distributed Systems
3. **Theory**: Algorithms, Complexity, Cryptography
4. **HCI**: Interaction design, social computing
5. **Graphics**: Rendering, animation, simulation
6. **Security**: Cryptography, network security
7. **Computational Biology**: Genomics, bioinformatics

**Funding Package**:
- Tuition: Fully covered (~$60,000/year)
- Stipend: $45,000-$50,000/year
- Health Insurance: Cardinal Care
- Conference Travel: Funded

**Career Outcomes**:
- **Academia (40-50%)**: Tenure-track faculty at top universities
- **Industry Research (30-40%)**: Google, Meta, Microsoft Research, OpenAI
- **Industry Engineering (10-20%)**: Senior/Staff Engineer at FAANG
- **Entrepreneurship (5-10%)**: Startup founders/CTOs

**Typical Salaries**:
- Academic Faculty: $120,000-$200,000 (starting)
- Industry Research Scientist: $180,000-$300,000 (base)
- Total Compensation: $330,000-$950,000 (with stock/bonus)

## 🎯 Learning Paths

### Path 1: Software Engineering Career
**Goal**: Become a software engineer at a top tech company

**Undergraduate**:
1. CS106A → CS106B → CS107 → CS110
2. CS103 → CS109 → CS161
3. CS140 (Operating Systems)
4. CS144 (Networking)
5. CS143 (Compilers) or CS149 (Parallel Computing)
6. CS194 (Software Project)

**Skills**: C/C++, Python, Java, systems programming, algorithms
**Outcome**: Software Engineer at FAANG ($150,000-$220,000)

---

### Path 2: Machine Learning Engineer
**Goal**: Build and deploy ML models in industry

**Undergraduate**:
1. CS106A → CS106B → CS107
2. CS103 → CS109 → CS161
3. CS221 (AI Principles)
4. CS229 (Machine Learning)

**Master's**:
1. CS224N (NLP) or CS231N (Vision)
2. CS230 (Deep Learning)
3. CS234 (Reinforcement Learning)
4. CS246 (Mining Massive Datasets)

**Skills**: Python, PyTorch/TensorFlow, ML algorithms, deployment
**Outcome**: ML Engineer ($160,000-$250,000)

---

### Path 3: Research Scientist (PhD)
**Goal**: Conduct research in AI/ML

**Undergraduate**:
1. Core CS courses (CS106A/B, CS107, CS103, CS109, CS161)
2. CS221, CS229 (AI/ML foundation)
3. CS224N or CS231N (specialization)
4. Research experience (CS191, CURIS)

**PhD** (5-6 years):
1. Year 1: Coursework + research rotations
2. Year 2: Qualifying exam + focused research
3. Years 3-5: Publications + dissertation
4. Year 5-6: Job market + defense

**Skills**: Research methodology, paper writing, deep expertise
**Outcome**: Research Scientist ($180,000-$300,000) or Faculty

---

### Path 4: Startup Founder/CTO
**Goal**: Start a tech company

**Undergraduate**:
1. Core CS courses
2. CS147 (HCI) - understand users
3. CS194 (Software Project) - build products
4. CS221, CS229 (AI/ML for product features)
5. Business courses (optional)

**Master's** (optional):
1. Specialized courses in area of interest
2. CS210 (Software Project Experience)
3. Entrepreneurship courses

**Skills**: Full-stack development, product sense, leadership
**Outcome**: Startup Founder/CTO (equity-based compensation)

---

### Path 5: Systems Engineer
**Goal**: Build large-scale distributed systems

**Undergraduate**:
1. CS106A → CS106B → CS107 → CS110
2. CS140 (Operating Systems)
3. CS144 (Networking)
4. CS145 (Databases)
5. CS149 (Parallel Computing)

**Master's**:
1. CS240 (Advanced OS)
2. CS244 (Advanced Networking)
3. CS244B (Distributed Systems)
4. CS245 (Data-Intensive Systems)

**Skills**: C/C++, distributed systems, performance optimization
**Outcome**: Systems Engineer ($160,000-$240,000)

## 📚 Essential Resources

### Must-Read Textbooks

#### Programming & Software Engineering
1. **The C Programming Language** - Kernighan & Ritchie
2. **Programming Abstractions in C++** - Eric Roberts
3. **Design Patterns** - Gang of Four

#### Systems
1. **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective** - Bryant & O'Hallaron
2. **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** - Arpaci-Dusseau
3. **Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach** - Kurose & Ross

#### Algorithms & Theory
1. **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** - Cormen et al.
2. **Algorithm Design** - Kleinberg & Tardos
3. **Introduction to the Theory of Computation** - Sipser

#### AI & Machine Learning
1. **Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach** - Russell & Norvig
2. **Deep Learning** - Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville
3. **Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning** - Bishop
4. **Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction** - Sutton & Barto

#### Mathematics
1. **Linear Algebra and Its Applications** - Strang
2. **Probability and Statistics** - DeGroot & Schervish
3. **Discrete Mathematics** - Rosen

## 💼 Career Information

### Industry Sectors

#### Tech Giants (FAANG+)
- **Companies**: Google, Meta, Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, Netflix
- **Roles**: Software Engineer, ML Engineer, Research Scientist
- **Compensation**: $150,000-$500,000+ (total comp)
- **Culture**: Fast-paced, innovative, competitive

#### AI Startups
- **Companies**: OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere, Hugging Face, Scale AI
- **Roles**: Research Scientist, ML Engineer, Founding Engineer
- **Compensation**: $150,000-$400,000 + equity
- **Culture**: Mission-driven, cutting-edge, high-growth

#### Autonomous Vehicles
- **Companies**: Waymo, Cruise, Tesla, Aurora, Zoox
- **Roles**: Perception Engineer, Planning Engineer, ML Engineer
- **Compensation**: $180,000-$350,000
- **Culture**: Hardware+software, safety-critical

#### Finance & Trading
- **Companies**: Jane Street, Citadel, Two Sigma, DE Shaw
- **Roles**: Quantitative Researcher, Software Engineer
- **Compensation**: $200,000-$500,000+ (base + bonus)
- **Culture**: Analytical, competitive, high-pressure

#### Academia
- **Positions**: Assistant Professor, Postdoc, Research Scientist
- **Institutions**: Top CS departments, research labs
- **Compensation**: $120,000-$250,000 (faculty)
- **Culture**: Research freedom, teaching, slower pace
