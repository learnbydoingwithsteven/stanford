# Stanford CS Master's Program Guide

**Complete Guide to MS in Computer Science**

---

## 🎓 Program Overview

The Stanford MS in Computer Science offers advanced study in computing with flexible specialization options. Students can pursue research-oriented or professional tracks.

### Program Options
1. **MS (45 units)**: Standard program, thesis optional
2. **MS with Specialization**: Focused depth in one area
3. **MS with Distinction in Research**: Thesis required
4. **Coterminal MS**: For Stanford undergrads (BS + MS)

### Duration
- **Full-time**: 1-2 years (typically 3-4 quarters)
- **Part-time**: 2-3 years (for employed students)

---

## 📋 Degree Requirements

### Total Units: 45

#### 1. **Foundations (10 units minimum)**
Must demonstrate proficiency in:
- **Logic, Automata & Complexity** (CS103)
- **Probability** (CS109, STATS 116, or equivalent)
- **Algorithmic Analysis** (CS161)
- **Computer Organization & Systems** (CS107)
- **Principles of Computer Systems** (CS110)

*Can be satisfied by undergraduate courses or graduate equivalents*

#### 2. **Breadth Requirements (3 courses from different areas)**

**Area A: Mathematical and Theoretical Foundations**
- CS154 - Automata and Complexity Theory
- CS157 - Computational Logic
- CS168 - Modern Algorithmic Toolbox
- CS254 - Computational Complexity
- CS261 - Optimization and Algorithmic Paradigms
- CS265 - Randomized Algorithms and Probabilistic Analysis

**Area B: Computer Systems**
- CS140 - Operating Systems
- CS143 - Compilers
- CS144 - Introduction to Computer Networking
- CS149 - Parallel Computing
- CS240 - Advanced Topics in Operating Systems
- CS244 - Advanced Topics in Networking

**Area C: Applications**
- CS145 - Data Management and Data Systems
- CS147 - Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction
- CS148 - Introduction to Computer Graphics
- CS221 - Artificial Intelligence: Principles and Techniques
- CS223A - Introduction to Robotics
- CS224N - Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning
- CS231N - Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition

**Area D: Computing and Society**
- CS181 - Computers, Ethics, and Public Policy
- CS182 - Ethics, Public Policy, and Technological Change
- CS256 - Computational Complexity and Society

#### 3. **Depth Requirements (21 units minimum)**
Choose one specialization area and complete:
- At least 21 units of depth courses (letter-graded)
- At least 15 units at 200-level or higher
- Maintain B average or better in depth courses

#### 4. **Electives (remaining units)**
- Can be any CS or related courses
- Up to 9 units can be from outside CS
- Research units (CS390) count as electives

## 🎯 Application Process

### Requirements
1. **Bachelor's Degree**: In CS or related field
2. **GPA**: Typically 3.5+ (unofficial minimum)
3. **GRE**: Not required (as of 2024)
4. **Letters of Recommendation**: 3 required
5. **Statement of Purpose**: Research interests and goals
6. **Transcripts**: Official transcripts from all universities
7. **Resume/CV**: Academic and professional experience

### Timeline
- **Application Deadline**: December 1 (for Autumn admission)
- **Decision Notification**: March-April
- **Acceptance Deadline**: April 15

### Admission Statistics
- **Acceptance Rate**: ~5-7%
- **Average GPA**: 3.8+
- **International Students**: ~40%
- **Women**: ~30%

## 💰 Funding & Financial Aid

### Tuition (2024-2025)
- **Per Unit**: ~$1,800
- **Total Program**: ~$81,000 (45 units)
- **Living Expenses**: ~$30,000/year

### Funding Options
1. **Teaching Assistantships (TA)**:
   - 8-10 hours/week
   - Tuition coverage + stipend (~$8,000/quarter)
   - Competitive, typically for 2nd year students

2. **Research Assistantships (RA)**:
   - Work with faculty on research
   - Tuition coverage + stipend
   - Requires faculty sponsor

3. **Fellowships**:
   - Stanford Graduate Fellowship (SGF)
   - External fellowships (NSF, NDSEG, etc.)
   - Full tuition + stipend

4. **Industry Sponsorship**:
   - Some companies sponsor employees
   - Part-time enrollment option

5. **Loans**:
   - Federal student loans
   - Private loans

### Cost-Saving Strategies
- **Coterminal Program**: Use undergrad financial aid
- **Part-time Enrollment**: Work while studying
- **Summer Internships**: Earn $20,000-$40,000
- **TA Positions**: Apply for 2nd year

## 💡 Tips for Success

### Academic
- **Plan Early**: Map out courses in first quarter
- **Balance Load**: Don't overload (9-12 units typical)
- **Seek Advising**: Meet with advisors regularly
- **Research Early**: Start research in first year if interested in PhD

### Professional
- **Internships**: Summer internships highly valuable
- **Networking**: Attend tech talks and career fairs
- **Projects**: Build portfolio of projects
- **Open Source**: Contribute to open-source projects

### Social
- **Join Clubs**: Connect with other students
- **Attend Events**: Department seminars and social events
- **Work-Life Balance**: Don't burn out
- **Explore Bay Area**: Take advantage of location

## 🔄 Program Variations

### MS with Distinction in Research
- **Requirements**: Same as regular MS + thesis
- **Thesis**: 9 units of CS390 (research)
- **Defense**: Oral defense of thesis
- **Benefits**: Preparation for PhD, deeper research experience

### Coterminal MS
- **Eligibility**: Stanford undergrads with 3.5+ GPA
- **Application**: Apply during junior or senior year
- **Units**: 45 total (some overlap with BS)
- **Duration**: Typically 1 additional year

### Part-Time MS
- **Enrollment**: 8-10 units per quarter
- **Duration**: 2-3 years
- **Benefits**: Work while studying
- **Restrictions**: Limited TA/RA opportunities
