## 📚 By Subject Area

### Programming & Software Engineering

#### **CS106A/B - Programming Fundamentals**

1. **The Art and Science of Java** by Eric Roberts
   - **Used in**: CS106A
   - **Topics**: Java programming basics, OOP
   - **Level**: Beginner
   - **Cost**: $80-100
   - **Alternatives**: Free online Java tutorials

2. **Programming Abstractions in C++** by Eric Roberts
   - **Used in**: CS106B
   - **Topics**: Data structures, recursion, C++
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $90-110
   - **Available**: Stanford bookstore, online

3. **The C Programming Language** by Kernighan & Ritchie
   - **Used in**: CS107
   - **Topics**: C programming, pointers, memory
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $50-70
   - **Status**: Classic, must-read
   - **ISBN**: 978-0131103627

### Systems

#### **CS107/110 - Computer Systems**

1. **Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective (CS:APP)** by Bryant & O'Hallaron
   - **Used in**: CS107
   - **Topics**: Computer architecture, assembly, memory, linking
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $100-130
   - **Edition**: 3rd edition (2015)
   - **ISBN**: 978-0134092669
   - **Why Essential**: Industry standard for systems programming
   - **Companion Site**: [csapp.cs.cmu.edu](http://csapp.cs.cmu.edu)

2. **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces** by Arpaci-Dusseau
   - **Used in**: CS110, CS111, CS140
   - **Topics**: OS concepts, processes, memory, file systems
   - **Level**: Intermediate to Advanced
   - **Cost**: FREE online
   - **Website**: [ostep.org](http://ostep.org)
   - **Why Essential**: Modern, accessible OS textbook
   - **Format**: PDF chapters available free

#### **CS140 - Operating Systems**

1. **Modern Operating Systems** by Andrew Tanenbaum
   - **Used in**: CS140
   - **Topics**: OS design, implementation, case studies
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $120-150
   - **Edition**: 4th edition
   - **ISBN**: 978-0133591620

#### **CS143 - Compilers**

1. **Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools** (Dragon Book) by Aho et al.
   - **Used in**: CS143
   - **Topics**: Lexing, parsing, optimization, code generation
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $100-130
   - **Edition**: 2nd edition
   - **ISBN**: 978-0321486813
   - **Nickname**: "Dragon Book" (purple dragon on cover)

2. **Engineering a Compiler** by Cooper & Torczon
   - **Alternative to**: Dragon Book
   - **Topics**: Modern compiler design
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $110-140
   - **Edition**: 2nd edition

#### **CS144 - Computer Networking**

1. **Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach** by Kurose & Ross
   - **Used in**: CS144
   - **Topics**: Internet protocols, TCP/IP, routing, applications
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $120-150
   - **Edition**: 8th edition (2021)
   - **ISBN**: 978-0135928615
   - **Why Essential**: Most popular networking textbook
   - **Companion Site**: Practice problems and Wireshark labs

2. **TCP/IP Illustrated, Volume 1** by Stevens
   - **Supplementary**: CS144
   - **Topics**: TCP/IP protocol details
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $60-80
   - **Status**: Classic reference

#### **CS145 - Databases**

1. **Database System Concepts** by Silberschatz, Korth & Sudarshan
   - **Used in**: CS145
   - **Topics**: SQL, relational algebra, transactions, indexing
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $130-160
   - **Edition**: 7th edition
   - **ISBN**: 978-0078022159

2. **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** by Martin Kleppmann
   - **Supplementary**: CS145, CS245
   - **Topics**: Modern distributed databases, scalability
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $50-70
   - **Why Essential**: Modern perspective on data systems
   - **Available**: O'Reilly

### Algorithms & Theory

#### **CS103 - Mathematical Foundations**

1. **Introduction to the Theory of Computation** by Michael Sipser
   - **Used in**: CS103, CS154
   - **Topics**: Automata, computability, complexity
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $100-130
   - **Edition**: 3rd edition
   - **ISBN**: 978-1133187790
   - **Why Essential**: Best introduction to theory of computation

2. **Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications** by Kenneth Rosen
   - **Supplementary**: CS103
   - **Topics**: Logic, proofs, sets, graphs, combinatorics
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $150-180
   - **Edition**: 8th edition

#### **CS109 - Probability**

1. **Introduction to Probability** by Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis
   - **Used in**: CS109
   - **Topics**: Probability theory, random variables, distributions
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $80-100
   - **Edition**: 2nd edition
   - **ISBN**: 978-1886529236
   - **Why Essential**: Clear explanations, good for CS students

2. **Probability and Statistics** by DeGroot & Schervish
   - **Alternative**: CS109
   - **Topics**: Probability, statistical inference
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: $150-180
   - **Edition**: 4th edition

#### **CS161 - Algorithms**

1. **Algorithm Design** by Kleinberg & Tardos
   - **Used in**: CS161
   - **Topics**: Greedy, divide-and-conquer, dynamic programming, graphs
   - **Level**: Intermediate to Advanced
   - **Cost**: $100-130
   - **ISBN**: 978-0321295354
   - **Why Essential**: Best undergraduate algorithms textbook
   - **Style**: Intuitive explanations, great examples

2. **Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)** by Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest & Stein
   - **Alternative**: CS161, used in CS261
   - **Topics**: Comprehensive algorithms coverage
   - **Level**: Advanced
   - **Cost**: $100-130
   - **Edition**: 4th edition (2022)
   - **ISBN**: 978-0262046305
   - **Nickname**: "CLRS"
   - **Why Essential**: Comprehensive reference, more formal than Kleinberg/Tardos

3. **Algorithms** by Dasgupta, Papadimitriou & Vazirani
   - **Alternative**: CS161
   - **Topics**: Algorithms with focus on intuition
   - **Level**: Intermediate
   - **Cost**: FREE online
   - **Website**: Available as free PDF
   - **Why Useful**: Concise, intuitive explanations

## 💰 Cost-Saving Strategies

### Free Textbooks
Many excellent CS textbooks are available free online:
- **Deep Learning** (Goodfellow et al.)
- **Reinforcement Learning** (Sutton & Barto)
- **Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces**
- **Elements of Statistical Learning**
- **Convex Optimization** (Boyd & Vandenberghe)
- **Speech and Language Processing** (Jurafsky & Martin)
- **Algorithms** (Dasgupta et al.)

### Alternatives to Buying New
1. **Library**: Stanford libraries have most textbooks
2. **Used Books**: Amazon, Chegg, campus bookstore
3. **International Editions**: Often much cheaper
4. **Older Editions**: Usually 90% the same content
5. **Rental**: Chegg, Amazon, campus bookstore
6. **Digital**: Often cheaper than print
7. **Share**: Split cost with classmates

### Stanford Resources
- **Green Library**: Reserve desk has course textbooks
- **Engineering Library**: CS-specific resources
- **Course Reserves**: Professors put copies on reserve
- **Digital Access**: Some textbooks via Stanford libraries
